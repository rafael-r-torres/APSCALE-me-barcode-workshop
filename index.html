Multi-Marker Metabarcoding Pipeline

Welcome to the dedicated training portal for our molecular ecology research group. This documentation will guide you through our standard computational workflow from raw sequence reads to ecological data analysis.

From Sequences to Ecology

This pipeline is designed to be fully reproducible, modular, and robust. We use environmental DNA (eDNA) and multi-marker approaches (COI, 18S, 16S) for marine biodiversity and aquaculture monitoring.

Workflow Roadmap

Our pipeline is broken down into three core modules. Each step requires specific inputs and generates outputs that feed directly into the next phase of the analysis.

1. APSCALE Processing

Raw fastq processing, denoising/clustering, and initial taxonomic assignment using local databases.

2. DADA2 & RDP in R

Advanced statistical denoising using DADA2 and robust taxonomic classification using the RDP Classifier algorithm.

3. Cloud Reproducibility

Executing heavy computations on Kaggle to bypass local hardware limits, and generating reproducible figures.

Why This Architecture?

By standardizing our workflow across these specific computational tools, we achieve three main goals:

  • Consistency: Everyone in the lab uses identical parameters, thresholds, and database configurations for comparable multi-project results.
  • Scalability: Pushing heavy sequence-clustering tasks to Kaggle prevents local machine crashes and standardizes the computing environment.
  • Flexibility: The modularity ensures the pipeline works seamlessly whether you are analyzing COI for metazoans, 18S for eukaryotes, or 16S for bacteria.
